Key Events Timeline

Explore the critical moments leading up to and following the assassination of President John F. Kennedy.

November 21, 1963

Arrival in Texas

President Kennedy arrives in Texas for a political trip meant to secure votes in the South for the 1964 election.

November 22, 1963 - 12:30 PM

Assassination

President Kennedy is fatally shot while riding in a presidential motorcade through Dealey Plaza in Dallas, Texas.

November 22, 1963 - 1:00 PM

Lee Harvey Oswald Arrest

Suspected assassin Lee Harvey Oswald is arrested at the Texas Theatre after allegedly shooting Dallas police officer J.D. Tippit.

November 24, 1963

Oswald Murdered

Oswald is shot and killed by nightclub owner Jack Ruby while being transferred from city jail to county jail on live television.

November 29, 1963

Warren Commission Established

President Lyndon B. Johnson creates the Warren Commission to investigate the assassination of President Kennedy.

September 24, 1964

Warren Report Released

The Commission's report concludes that Oswald acted alone in the assassination, a finding disputed by many researchers.
